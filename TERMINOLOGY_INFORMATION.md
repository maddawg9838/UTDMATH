# Project Terminology & Information

- **Birkeland Currents**: The currents that flow along the magnetic field due to the interaction between the solar wind and the Earth's magnetosphere, which flow through space and along lines of magnetic force
- **Cross-Tail Current**: One portion of the current system that flows in a sheet across the center of the magnetotail, dividing the tail into two lobes (low-density regions) of oppositely directed magnetic field
- **Plasma**: Ionized gas which consists of a mix of free electrons and positively charged ions
- **Reconnection**: A process by which magnetic energy is converted into plasma energy, due to the interconnection of oppositely directed magnetic fields
  - When the IMF has a southward component, reconnection occurs on the dayside, and the magnetic lines of force in the solar wind become connected with the lines of force from the Earth
  - Transfers magnetic flux into the magnetotail, which is equivalent to storing energy in the magnetotail
- **Solar Wind**: A tenuous (very weak or slight), rapidly flowing plasma
- **Substorm**: The process by which energy is released from the magnetosphere
