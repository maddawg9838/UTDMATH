# Correlations between Plasma Flows and Magnetic Structures in the Magnetosphere

**Abstract**: Fast flows of hot ions from the near-Earth reconnection region are a signature feature of the onset of magnetosphere substorm and a key component of plasma and energetic particle transport (in the form of injections) from the night-side magnetosphere into the inner magnetosphere, where these particles populate radiation belts and ring current. Detection of such flows by magnetosphere missions like THEMIS and MMS is often used for monitoring in-situ geomagnetic conditions and for predicting plasma injections. 

---

**THEMIS**: Time History of Events and Macroscale Interactions during Substorms
- Purpose: Studies how mass and energy from the solar wind are transferred into Earth's magnetosphere, to understand the physical processes that trigger magnetospheric substorms, space weather processes, and the Sun-Earth interactions.
  - **Magnetospheric Substorms**: Sudden releases of energy within Earth's magnetosphere that intensify auroral activity near the polar regions.
- Method: 5 identical satellites (THA, THB, THC, THD, & THE) were placed in coordinated orbits around Earth, allowing scientists to measure magnetic fields, electric fields, and charged particles simultaneously at different locations within the magnetosphere and magnetotail.
- Outcome: By comparing measurements from multiple satellites, researchers can determine where substorms begin and how energy propagates through Earth's magnetic environment.

<br>

**ARTEMIS**: Acceleration, Reconnection, Turbulence, and Electrodynamics of the Moon's Interaction with the Sun
- Purpose: Satellites in lunar orbits study how the solar wind interacts with the Moon, to understand lunar plasma wake, particle acceleration processes, & the interaction between Earth's magnetotail and the Moon's weak magnetic field.
- Method: Satellites THB and THC were repurposed to collect data from opposite sides of the Moon, allowing scientists to measure particles and electromagnetic fields simultaneously at different sides of the Moon. 
- Outcome: Scientists were able to construct 3D maps of plasma dynamics and energetic particle acceleration near the Moon's orbit. Revealed that the lunar plasma wake can extend roughly 12 lunar radii behind the Moon and can distort the interplanetary magnetic field.

<br>

**Satellite Equipment**: All THEMIS and ARTEMIS spacecraft carry identical instruments, allowing consistent measurements across the Earth-Moon system
- Fluxgate Magnetometer (FGM): Measure background magnetic fields
- Search-Coil Magnetometer (SCM): Detect magnetic field fluctuations
- Electric Field Instrument (EFI): Measure electric fields and plasma density
- Electrostatic Analyzer (ESA): Measure thermal ions and electrons
- Solid State Telescope (SST): Measure high-energy particles
