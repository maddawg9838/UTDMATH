# Correlations between Plasma Dlows and Magnetic Structures in the Magnetosphere

##**Abstract**##: Fast flows of hot ions from the near-Earth reconnection region are a signature element of onset of magnetosphere substorm and a key component of plasma and energetic particle transport (in a form of injections) from the night-side magnetosphere into the inner magnetosphere, where these particles populate radiation belts and ring current. Detection of such flows by magnetosphere missions like THEMIS and MMS are often used for monitoring in-situ geomagnetic conditions and for predicting plasma injections.
